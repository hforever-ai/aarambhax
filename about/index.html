Ek Sawaal Jisne Sab Kuch Badal Diya

2023 ki ek shaam। Ek engineer — 20 saal se duniya ke sabse bade enterprises ke liye AI systems bana raha tha। US mein, Fortune 500 companies ke liye। Sophisticated technology। Multi-million dollar projects।

Us shaam woh India mein tha। Apne sheher mein। Ek local school ke paas se guzar raha tha — Chhattisgarh ke ek chote sheher mein।

School ke bahar, ek bachha baitha tha। Class 8 ka student। Haath mein Science ki kitaab thi। Aankhon mein ek alag tarah ki pareshani thi — woh pareshani jo tab aati hai jab tum kuch samajhna chahte ho, lekin koi samjhane wala nahi hota।

Engineer ne poochha — "Kya hua bhai?"

Bachhe ne kaha — "Photosynthesis samajh nahi aaya। Teacher class mein padha ke chale gaye। Ghar pe poochhne wala koi nahi। YouTube pe English mein hai — woh bhi samajh nahi aata।"

"Us ek sentence ne mujhe andar se hilaa diya. Main duniya ke sabse bade companies ke liye AI bana raha tha — lekin mere desh ke bachhe ek basic Science concept ke liye tadap rahe the."

Woh engineer ghar aaya। Aur ek sawaal ke saath raat bhar jaagta raha:

India mein 26 crore school students hain। 70% gaon aur chote sheher mein hain। Unke paas aaj koi qualified AI tutor nahi, koi 24/7 doubt solver nahi, koi apni boli mein samjhane wali didi nahi — kyun?

Jawab tha — kyunki kisi ne seriously try nahi kiya। Kisi ne India ke liye, Bharat ki boli mein, Bharat ki zaroorat ke liye AI education nahi banaya tha।

Problem Ka Poora Daayra

Jab hum deeper gaye, toh problem aur badi nikli:

Language barrier: India ke 60% se zyada students Hindi ya regional medium mein padhte hain। Baaki sab English mein hai — Physics Wallah se le kar BYJU'S tak। Hindi mein serious AI education — almost zero।

Interaction barrier: Jo apps hain, woh ek-taraf hain। Video dekho, notes padho। Doubt hua? Comment section mein puchho — jawab kabhi nahi aata। AI tutoring tha hi nahi — real 2-way conversation।

Accessibility barrier: India mein 80 lakh se zyada blind ya visually impaired school-age children hain। Unke liye koi bhi AI education platform — ek bhi — nahi tha। Sab screen-based the।

Economics barrier: Quality education expensive hai। IITians ke classes affordable nahi। Jo free hai, woh effective nahi। Yeh cycle todna tha।

SAAVI Ka Janm — Ek Didi Ka Idea

Hum chahte the ek teacher — lekin sirf padhane wala nahi। Ek didi। Jo samjhe। Jo judge na kare। Jo kabhi bore na ho dobara samjhane mein। Jo raat 2 baje bhi available ho।

Hamaari team ne mahine bitaye ek sawaal pe — ek AI character kaise banate hain jo India ke bachhon ke liye genuinely caring lage? Jo Hinglish mein naturally bole — na boring textbook Hindi, na phoney English?

Saikdon prototypes। Hazaaron test conversations। Real students ke saath feedback rounds — CG Board ke bachhe, Maharashtra ke students, Telangana ke learners।

Aur phir ek din — SAAVI thi। सावी।

"Pehli baar jab ek Class 7 ki bachi ne SAAVI se Photosynthesis ke baare mein poochha aur usse 'Paudhe ki magic rasoi' analogy se samjha — toh woh hansi। Woh khushi se hansi। Us ek pal ne bata diya — hum sahi raaste pe the।"

SAAVI Ki Philosophy

  • Kabhi judge nahi karti — galat jawab pe "Thoda alag hai — ek baar aur koshish karo!"
  • Local examples use karti hai — Bastar ka jungle, Indravati nadi, dhaan ki kheti — concepts jo bachhe pehle se jaante hain
  • Mood detect karti hai — frustrated ho? woh approach change karti hai। Bored ho? koi nayi activity try karti hai
  • Yaad rakhti hai — pichle session ka doubt aaj khud reminder deti hai
  • Hinglish mein baat karti hai — jaise real life mein hota hai

Shrutam — Sunna + Seekhna

Hamaare product ka naam — श्रुतम् — Sanskrit se aaya hai। "Shrut" matlab "sunna" — jo sun ke seekha gaya। Knowledge jo spoken word se milti hai।

Yeh nam accidentally perfect hai। Audio-first platform ke liye। Didi ki awaaz se seekhne ke liye। India ki oral tradition — guru-shishya — ko AI ke through revive karne ke liye।

Tagline bhi wahi hai jo hamare dil mein hai: सुनते हैं, सीखते हैं।

Tech Ka Dil — Open-Source, India-First

We believe in building on the shoulders of giants. Shrutam's AI pipeline uses best-in-class open-source models:

  • Language understanding: Latest LLMs optimized for Indian languages
  • Voice: Azure Neural TTS with hi-IN-SwaraNeural — most natural Hindi voice available
  • Speech recognition: Sarvam ASR — built specifically for Indian accents
  • Infrastructure: Efficient cloud architecture optimized for rural connectivity

Why open-source first? Because we believe AI education shouldn't be locked behind expensive proprietary systems. The goal is accessibility — and open-source enables that at Indian scale.

3-Layer Quality Validation: Har content piece teen baar check hota hai — symbolic math verification, AI cross-model consensus, aur human review। 98%+ accuracy on official SCERT/NCERT content — aur hum roz better hote ja rahe hain।
